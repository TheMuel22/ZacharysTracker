What is weaning?

The gradual introduction of solid foods to a baby’s diet while reducing reliance on breast milk or formula.

Types of weaning?

Traditional Weaning: Parent-led spoon-feeding purees and mashed foods.

Baby-Led Weaning: Baby self-feeds with soft finger foods, skips purees.

Combination Feeding: A mix of both methods, which many families use in practice.

When to Start Weaning?

Most health authorities recommend starting around 6 months, however each baby is different and there are key milestones to watch out for.

  • WHO: Exclusive breastfeeding until 6 months, then complementary foods.
  • NHS (UK): Around 6 months, when babies show readiness signs.
  • AAP (US): Around 6 months, though some babies may be ready between 4–6 months.

Signs your Baby is Ready

  • Can sit upright with support.
  • Has good head and neck control.
  • Shows interest in food (grabbing, watching adults eat).
  • Can move food from the front of the mouth to the back to swallow.
  • Loss of the tongue-thrust reflex (no longer automatically pushes food out).
